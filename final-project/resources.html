Renting Tips & Tenant Rights

What to Look for When Renting:
  • Lease Terms - Understand all clauses and fees
  • Security Deposits - Know Idaho's deposit laws
  • Maintenance Responsibilities - Who fixes what?
  • Utility Arrangements - What's included vs. separate

Frequently Asked Questions

Student housing in Rexburg typically ranges from $300-600 per month for shared accommodations. Private bedrooms in student complexes average $400-500/month, while shared rooms can be as low as $250-350/month. Most student housing includes utilities and internet.

Most students prefer to live within 1-2 miles of campus for easy walking or biking. The campus shuttle system also serves many apartment complexes. Living within the "BYU-I approved housing" area ensures you meet university requirements if you're an unmarried student.

Monthly utilities in Rexburg typically cost: Electricity $80-120, Natural Gas $40-80 (higher in winter), Water/Sewer $30-50, Internet $30-60, and Trash $15-25. Total utilities for a 2-bedroom apartment average $200-350/month depending on usage and season.

While a car is helpful, it's not absolutely necessary. BYU-I provides free shuttle service to many student housing complexes. The city is bike-friendly, and many students walk or bike to campus. However, a car is convenient for grocery shopping and weekend activities.

For fall semester, start looking in February-March for the best selection. Spring semester housing becomes available in October-November. Summer housing is often easier to find and may have lower rates. Many leases follow the academic calendar.

Pet policies vary widely. Many student housing complexes don't allow pets, while family housing is more pet-friendly. Pet deposits typically range from $200-500, with monthly pet rent of $25-50. Always check pet policies before signing a lease.
